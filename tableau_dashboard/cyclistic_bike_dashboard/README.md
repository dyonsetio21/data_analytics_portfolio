## Project Overview
This project is part of an advanced portfolio in the **Tableau Data Visualization Series**.  
The dashboard explores rider behavior patterns using the **Cyclistic Bike-Share dataset**, aiming to understand differences between **casual riders** and **annual members**.  
It provides insights into ride volume, duration trends, and usage behavior across time and stations.

## Dashboard Overview
![Cyclistic Dashboard](https://github.com/dyonsetio21/data_analytics_portfolio/blob/main/tableau_dashboard/cyclistic_bike_dashboard/dashboard_image/Cyclistic%20Rider%20Behavior.png)

## Key Insights:
**1. Seasonal Ride Trends**
  - Ride volume peaks during the summer months, especially around June and July.
  - Casual riders show stronger seasonal fluctuations compared to members.

**2. Ride Duration Differences**
  - Casual riders take longer rides on average than members.
  - Members typically use bikes for shorter, routine commutes.

**3. Top Start Stations**
  - Popular stations are concentrated around tourist and downtown areas.
  - Casual riders dominate usage at lakeside and recreational spots.

**4. Weekday vs Weekend Patterns**
  - Members ride more frequently on weekdays, reflecting commute habits.
  - Casual riders are more active during weekends and holidays.

## Business Recommendations:
**1. Target Marketing for Casual Riders**
  - Offer seasonal promotions or discounts to encourage casual users to subscribe as members.

**2. Improve Member Retention**
  - Create loyalty programs or add benefits to maintain high weekday usage.

**3. Expand Station Coverage Strategically**
  - Consider adding or improving stations in high-demand tourist and leisure zones.

**4. Optimize Operations During Peak Seasons**
  - Allocate more bikes and maintenance staff during the summer months.
